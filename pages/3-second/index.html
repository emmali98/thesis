You were the second human I'd seen up close since I woke up. No, third.
And it was my first time seeing a child.

You looked so helpless.

The entire hike back to your home, you shivered even though my heating systems
were on. I suppose you were scared. You saw my face.

You didn't struggle, even though I said nothing to you about taking you back to
your family.

But I remember you saying thank you when we got to the edge of the camp.
Your voice . . . hearing it was painful. I thought I could go the rest of
eternity without hearing another voice, but your thank you reminded me that
I was lying to myself.

Why I kept reading all these books and watching those movies,
going through dead people's things and standing on that bridge.

I tried staying away to prove that I could even do it, but I was made for this, wasn't I?
To "protect and serve"? To love humanity to the point of murder? I probably would've
killed for you on that walk back to your home. Does that seem right?

Because I was made to care and to kill?
