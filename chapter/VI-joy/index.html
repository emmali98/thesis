I don't think you realize how cruel my time was.

How am I supposed
to explain it
to you?

Why are you angry?

Did you know the storms used to only
last hours, not weeks?

The sea was safe to swim in,
without poisoning your skin?

The cities were full of people and
life? Not so full of ghosts and
ruins and dead people's things?

Do you understand that the world
was broken? By people like him?

And even before these disasters happened,
could you understand the stupid luxuries
people invented for themselves,

even as they tortured and killed
millions elsewhere? The gluttony,
the greed? Senselessly cruel,
and wrapped up in pretty words.

"Life's not fair."

"Every man for himself."

"I earned this,
I'm a self-made man."

And when he and the rest of them
realized what they had done, what did
they do?

Did they try to mitigate the damage?
Did they take responsibility?

They ran away and hid.

When the air grew hot and choking,
they moved north and bought air
purifiers.

When people fell ill, they sold the
cure to other nations at a premium.

When the seas grew high and dangerous,
they sold their beach houses and
changed vacation plans.

And when people were angry,
when people were dying and the
disasters they had wrought couldn't
be held at bay with wealth,

they built me. They built
others like me, they built
bunkers, they built sleeping pods.

They built monsters like me
to protect themselves from the
people rightfully outraged at
their flagrant disregard for life,
for the world.

And when we woke up late . . .
long after all the other bunkers and
pods and robots were gone . . .

And when
he

awoke

awoke to
a

world
half-healed,

to a second life,

with an immortal protector at his side,

I saw the joy in his eyes.
