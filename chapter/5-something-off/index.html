Marvin likes to roam.

For the first couple years, I would trail him everywhere

"Lovely Marvin!"

I felt ashamed for not having named him,

And I recognized you immediately, of course.
Your DNA, but also your face. Your expression.
Your voice saying thank you and now calling
Marvin's name.

I know you're not like him.

I see how you treat Marvin. How you check him for fleas,
how you bring him treats, how you play with him,
despite not owning him. Despite not needing to help him.

I don't know why I stay in the city, near the bridge.

I know Marvin likes seeing you in the meadow a few miles inland.

I have the garden to keep.

I pay attention to the bridge,
so people don't fall,
and so they don't enter the city,
and hurt themselves in the ruins.

But I could just leave. I could leave Marvin with you,
let the garden rot,
let the bridge and people fall,

stop reading and watching and looking,

stop wearing the coat and hat,

stop everything.

And I don't know why I don't.
